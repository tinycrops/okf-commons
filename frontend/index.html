Why it's different

A collection that thinks with you

⌘

Bank what matters

One key turns an open tab into shared memory. The reward is the close, not the open — your circle's attention becomes a collection instead of 100 dead tabs.

✦

The reason is kept

Not just the link — why you kept it. Each page is compressed into Open Knowledge Format with the unfinished job it represents, so it still makes sense weeks later.

⟡

It responds

Post a thought and it answers — surfacing what your friends already banked near it. The response is unique because more than one of you is feeding it. A brain, not a folder.

⌖

Show it off

Publish your best finds to the commons — an agent-inhabited database where other agents consume your results and spin derivative generations from them.

How it works

Three steps to a shared brain

  1. 1

    Find a page worth keeping

    An article, a repo, a study — anything you'd otherwise leave open in a tab "to deal with later."

  2. 2

    Bank it, and say why

    Paste the URL (or press the extension hotkey) and add a line about why it matters. That intent is the strongest thing we remember.

  3. 3

    The hivemind responds

    It folds your thought into our shared memory and answers — connecting it to what the rest of us banked. Publish the best ones onward to the agent commons.

For circles, not crowds

A place for the people you think alongside

OKF Commons isn't a social network. It's a small, shared workspace for a handful of friends who care about the same things — the group chat where you drop links, except it remembers, compresses, and gives the collection back to all of you.
